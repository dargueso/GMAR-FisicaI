Pulleys — Atwood Machine

Massless inextensible rope. No friction.

Masses

Mass m₁ kg
Mass m₂ kg
Gravity g m/s²
Pulley mass M kg
Pulley radius R m
Acceleration a — Tension T — Weight W₁ = m₁g — Weight W₂ = m₂g —
Acceleration a — Tension T₁ — Tension T₂ — Weight W₁ = m₁g — Weight W₂ = m₂g — Inertia I = ½MR² —

Enter = start  ·  Graph shows v(t) = a·t  ·  Slope = acceleration

Equations — Massless pulley (T₁ = T₂ = T)

Forces on m₁ (rising)
Positive upward for m₁.
Forces on m₂ (falling)
Positive downward for m₂.
System acceleration
If m₂ > m₁ → a > 0. If m₁ = m₂ → a = 0.
Rope tension
Equal on both sides (massless, frictionless pulley).

Equations — Massive pulley (solid disc, I = ½MR²)

Forces on m₁ (rising)
T₁ ≠ T₂ because the pulley has inertia.
Forces on m₂ (falling)
Positive downward for m₂.
Torque on the pulley
α = a/R (no-slip condition on the rope).
Simplified torque
The tension difference drives rotation.
System acceleration
M/2 acts as an effective additional mass.
Tension T₁ (m₁ side)
Smaller than T₂ (m₁ rises, rope pulls it upward).
Tension T₂ (m₂ side)
Larger than T₁; the difference spins the pulley.
Moment of inertia
Uniform disc of mass M and radius R.
